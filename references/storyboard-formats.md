# Storyboard Formats Reference

Use this reference to choose panel count, sheet structure, and rhythm template.

## Panel Count Defaults

1. 6 panels
   Use for concept proof, poster like short arc, pitch thumbnail, or one simple action.

2. 8 panels
   Use for a short scene with setup, turn, and payoff.

3. 12 panels
   Use for most video generation prompts. It balances story clarity, camera variety, and prompt readability.

4. 16 panels
   Use for kinetic action, dance, sports, fight, chase, ritual, music video, or any scene where continuity of motion matters.

5. 24 panels
   Use for detailed animatic planning, choreography breakdowns, commercials with product beats, or complex multi scene sequences.

## Sheet Layouts

1. 12 panels
   Use a 4 by 3 grid for a landscape storyboard sheet. The first row establishes world and action language, the second row escalates, the third row resolves or explodes.

2. 16 panels
   Use a 4 by 4 grid for dense action. Make the final panel larger only if the user explicitly wants a dramatic final panel layout.

3. 8 panels
   Use a 4 by 2 grid for fast proof of concept, or 2 by 4 for vertical reading.

4. Separate frames
   Use when the user wants an animatic, video clip generation, or per shot prompts rather than one image sheet.

## General Rhythm Template

1. Establish location, scale, tone, and protagonist silhouette.
2. Introduce desire, task, performance, or threat.
3. Show first decisive movement.
4. Change camera position to reveal stakes or space.
5. Add obstacle, counterforce, or emotional complication.
6. Intensify motion or compress space.
7. Insert close detail that makes the action tactile.
8. Reverse, collapse, miss, dodge, or impossible transition.
9. Spatial reset from a new angle.
10. Peak acceleration or strongest emotional exposure.
11. Impact, release, revelation, or near stillness.
12. Final image with clear silhouette and aftershock.

For 16 panels, split escalation into smaller movement phases and add more continuity panels: windup, launch, midair, contact, rebound, slide, recovery, second launch, and final hold.

## Action Or Sports Template

Make the prop or performance object visible across the sequence. Use full body readable panels for technique, low angles for power, close ups for impact details, side tracking for speed, overhead panels for footwork, and final wormseye or low angle hero pose for release.

Required beats: stance, windup, first burst, impossible acceleration, obstacle, midair distortion, tactile close up, landing or recovery, second escalation, environmental rupture, slow motion apex, final aftershock.

## Dance Or Performance Template

Treat choreography as emotional narrative. Alternate full body silhouette panels with intimate face, mouth, hand, hair, and fabric details. Track breath, exhaustion, rhythm hits, floor contact, reflection, spotlight, and negative space.

Required beats: lonely entrance, first phrase, turn or slide, body isolation, vocal emphasis, collapse, floor transition, rebound, close emotional rupture, final sculptural pose.

## Horror Or Suspense Template

Use negative space, restricted visibility, delayed reveal, offscreen pressure, frame within frame, and a controlled alternation of stillness and movement. Do not reveal the threat too early unless the concept depends on visible pursuit.

Required beats: ordinary space, wrong detail, isolated reaction, unseen movement, blocked escape, false calm, close clue, sudden proximity, spatial reversal, final unresolved image.

## Dialogue Or Character Scene Template

Use fewer camera moves and stronger reaction logic. Preserve eyelines, screen direction, and emotional cause and effect. Let close ups arrive only after spatial context is clear.

Required beats: establishing relationship, speaker action, listener reaction, object or gesture insert, power shift, silence, final decision.

## Commercial Or Product Template

Keep product recognizability high. Use clear hero frames, macro inserts, user action, benefit visualization, contrast before and after, and final branded composition if branding is requested.

Required beats: problem, product entrance, tactile detail, use moment, transformation, social proof or result, final product hero image.

## Music Video Or Experimental Template

Let rhythm, texture, and motif drive continuity. Use repeated visual motifs, aggressive camera variety, stylized transitions, and emotional or lyric accents. The storyboard can be less literal, but every panel still needs a readable subject action.

Required beats: motif introduction, performance identity, first rhythm break, spatial distortion, close lyric or breath cue, repeated motif mutation, sensory overload, final iconic hold.

## Final Panel Options

1. Hero pose under isolated light.
2. Subject tiny in destroyed or transformed space.
3. Extreme close up with aftermath reflected in eye or object.
4. Overhead diagram of collapse or completion.
5. Silhouette frozen at the apex of motion.
6. Object left behind after action exits frame.
